Credit Management




Common Mistakes

• Believing that one single number (the credit score) tells the whole story—when in reality, lenders examine detailed credit reports.

• Not understanding that late payments, even by a few days, can have a disproportionate impact on credit scores.
• Misconceptions around “good debt” vs. “bad debt”: Some may assume all debt is harmful, whereas responsible borrowing for education or a home can build credit if managed well.

• Overlooking the importance of reading the fine print when choosing a credit card (e.g., hidden fees, interest rate changes, and credit limit conditions).

• Failing to monitor one’s credit report regularly to catch errors.
