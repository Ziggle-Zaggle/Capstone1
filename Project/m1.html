Key Terms and Definitions

• Credit Management: The process of assessing creditworthiness, setting credit limits, monitoring accounts receivable, and managing collections to minimize risk and ensure healthy cash flow.

• Interest Rates: The percentage charged on a loan or earned on an investment, which affects how quickly debt can grow.

• Inflation: The general increase in prices over time that can erode purchasing power and affect loan repayment costs.

• Credit Rating System: Agencies like Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion use financial data to generate credit reports and scores.

• Credit Score: A numerical representation (often 300–850) of a person’s creditworthiness based on their credit history, outstanding debt, payment patterns, etc.

• Factors Influencing Credit Score: Payment history, credit utilization ratio, length of credit history, new credit inquiries, and credit mix.

• Late Payments: Payments made after the due date; these can severely lower your credit score.

• Good vs. Bad Debt: Good debt (e.g., student loans, mortgages) is often an investment in the future, whereas bad debt (e.g., high-interest credit cards with only minimum payments) can trap you in a cycle of debt.

• Choosing a Credit Card: Compare factors such as annual fees, rewards, interest rates, and credit limits; for many, a “first” credit card should offer manageable limits and low fees.

Common Mistakes

• Believing that one single number (the credit score) tells the whole story—when in reality, lenders examine detailed credit reports.

• Not understanding that late payments, even by a few days, can have a disproportionate impact on credit scores.
• Misconceptions around “good debt” vs. “bad debt”: Some may assume all debt is harmful, whereas responsible borrowing for education or a home can build credit if managed well.

• Overlooking the importance of reading the fine print when choosing a credit card (e.g., hidden fees, interest rate changes, and credit limit conditions).

• Failing to monitor one’s credit report regularly to catch errors.
